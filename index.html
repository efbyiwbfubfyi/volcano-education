VOLCANO




          A volcano is a natural geological formation, usually in the shape of a mountain or hill, that forms when magma, gases, and molten rock escape from beneath the Earth's crust. Volcanoes are openings or vents in the Earth's surface that allow hot materials from inside the Earth to reach the surface. Over time, repeated eruptions can build a large structure made of solidified lava, ash, and volcanic rocks. Volcanoes are an important part of Earth's geology because they contribute to the creation of new land, release gases into the atmosphere, and affect the global climate.



          Volcanoes form mainly at the boundaries of tectonic plates, where plates collide, move apart, or slide past each other. When plates move, they create fractures in the Earth's crust. Magma from the mantle rises through these fractures because it is less dense than the surrounding solid rock. As the magma reaches the surface, it erupts as lava, ash, and gas, gradually forming a volcano. Some volcanoes can also form over “hotspots” in the middle of tectonic plates, where magma rises from deep within the mantle.



There are 5 parts of a volcano is:

  1. Magma Chamber: A reservoir of molten rock beneath the Earth's surface.
  2. Vent: The main opening where magma exits.
  3. Crater: A bowl-shaped depression at the top of the volcano, formed around the vent.
  4. Lava Flow: Streams of molten rock that pour out during an eruption.
  5. Ash Cloud: Fine particles of rock and glass ejected into the atmosphere during explosive eruptions.



There are 2 types of eruption is:

  • Explosive eruptions: Powerful explosions that eject lava, ash, and gases high into the air. These can cause pyroclastic flows and ashfall.
  • Effusive eruptions: Gentle lava flows that gradually spread across the land.



There are a several types of volcano mountain that is famous is:

  • Mount Merapi in Indonesia – one of the most active volcanoes in the world.
  • Mount Vesuvius in Italy – famous for the AD 79 eruption that buried Pompeii.
  • Mount Fuji in Japan – an iconic and dormant volcano.
